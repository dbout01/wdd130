Christmas Cookies & Wine Pairings

Wanting to impress your friends, or maybe your in-laws during the Christmas season? Here's a guide to help you step-up your wine & appetizer game

Cabernet Sauvignon & Peppermint

Peppermint cookies pair well with bold reds. The richness of the wine accentuates the herbal peppermint taste without being too overbearing.

Riesling & Gingerbread

The light wine that is Riesling makes a good contrast with the spices of gingerbread. The oily texture of the wine seamlessly rolls over the rough texture of the gingerbread.

Prosecco & Sugar Cookies

The acidity clashes with the sugar, but in a good way. The fun bubbles roll along your tongue will savouring the sweet and smooth cookie.

Pinot Noir & Jam Thumbprint

Pinot Noir can range from light to medium red, and it pairs well the with fruity and sweet flavours that make up jam thumbprint cookies. The way pinot noir is age will surely add a bit of sweetness to an otherwise tart taste.

Chardonnay & Shortbread

Chardonnay, being famous for its buttery-smooth flavour, pairs great with the buttery-consistency of shortbread cookie. It's surely a match made in Heaven!

Merlot & Chocolate Chip

The rich, fruit flavour of merlot pairs well with the decadent yet sugar flavour of chocolate chip cookies. This makes for a great dessert pairing as well as sweet treat on the side of some roast.

Syrah & Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

Syrah is known to be consistently 'spicy' and rich. It can overwhelm any other dessert except for oatmeal raisin cookies. The two seem to blend into one flavour.

Port & Peanut Butter Cookies

Peanut butter, known for it's outstanding and rich flavour needs a sweet wine to complement it. Port, having hints of nutty flavours but going light on sweetness, goes well with peanut butter cookies.
